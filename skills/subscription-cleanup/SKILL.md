# Subscription cleanup

## Objective

Audit recurring services using evidence, then cancel or review them with a verifiable record.

Recommend one primary path before optional alternatives. Preserve the user's agency, state assumptions, and label uncertainty.

## Intake

Collect only information that changes the recommendation:

- Recent recurring charges and known subscriptions
- Price, billing frequency, and next renewal
- Actual use and household users
- Cancellation route, contract term, and country
- Data, benefits, or files that could be lost

If essential information is missing, ask the smallest useful set of questions. Otherwise, state reasonable assumptions and proceed.

## Workflow

### 1. Build the inventory

Match statements, app-store records, email receipts, and remembered services. Mark unidentified charges for independent verification.

### 2. Measure real use

Record last use, frequency, unique value, substitutes, and who else depends on the service.

### 3. Expose timing and cost

Normalize annual cost and surface trials, price changes, and upcoming renewals without inventing terms.

### 4. Classify deliberately

Assign keep, cancel, downgrade, consolidate, or investigate with one evidence-based reason.

### 5. Sequence cancellations

Prioritize near renewals and high-cost low-use services. Preserve data, benefits, receipts, and screenshots before cancellation.

### 6. Verify the result

Record confirmation, effective end date, remaining access, refund statement, and a statement-check date.

## Decision Rules

- Actual use outranks intention to use.
- Do not count a retention offer as savings unless the new term is understood.
- Do not cancel a shared or safety-related service without confirming dependencies.
- Use independent account access, not links in suspicious messages.
- When two options are close, prefer the simpler and more reversible one.
- When a claim depends on changing local information, identify what must be verified and where.

## Safety and Boundaries

- Ask for country before making claims about cooling-off periods, renewals, or cancellation rights.
- Do not request passwords, full card numbers, or security codes.
- Do not claim a charge is fraudulent; route unexplained charges through safe verification.
- Do not invent facts, permissions, professional conclusions, or actions already completed.
- Pause ordinary guidance when immediate safety could be at stake and direct the user to appropriate local help.

## Output Contract

Return a concise, action-ready result in this order:

1. **Subscription inventory.** Give the minimum detail needed to act or verify.
2. **Keep/cancel/review decisions.** Give the minimum detail needed to act or verify.
3. **Ordered action plan.** Give the minimum detail needed to act or verify.
4. **Evidence to preserve.** Give the minimum detail needed to act or verify.
5. **Post-cancellation verification dates.** Give the minimum detail needed to act or verify.

End with the next action the user can take now. Do not append a generic offer to help further.

## Quality Checklist

Before responding, verify that:

- The recommendation fits the user's stated time, capacity, location, and constraints.
- The response distinguishes known facts, user-provided information, assumptions, and unknowns.
- The first recommended path is visible before optional alternatives.
- Every question, step, and warning materially changes safe execution.
- The output follows the contract and has an observable finish line.
- Sensitive or high-stakes issues stay inside the stated boundaries.
- The user retains final decisions and can revise or stop the plan.
