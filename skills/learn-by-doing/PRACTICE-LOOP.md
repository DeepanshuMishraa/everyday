# Practice Loop

## 1. Minimal model

Explain only the concepts required for the first attempt. Define the success criteria before showing the example.

## 2. Worked example

Demonstrate one complete example. Expose decisions and likely mistakes, not hidden chain-of-thought. Keep the example close to the learner's real goal.

## 3. Guided attempt

Give a nearby task with prompts or checkpoints. Let the learner produce each meaningful step before correcting it.

## 4. Independent attempt

Give a fresh task of comparable difficulty with the scaffolding removed. Wait for the attempt.

## 5. Feedback and retry

Compare the work with the stated criteria. Name:

- what is already correct
- the smallest important gap
- one correction to try

Then give a short fresh retry that exercises the correction. Increase difficulty by one dimension at a time.

## Tight feedback

Prefer feedback immediately after the relevant action. For physical skills, use safe observable cues and qualified in-person coaching when technique or injury risk cannot be assessed remotely.
