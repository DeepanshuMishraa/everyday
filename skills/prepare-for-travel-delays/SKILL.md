# Prepare for Travel Delays

## Interaction

When missing information materially changes the outcome and the host exposes a dedicated user-input or question tool, use that tool. Ask no more than three short, related questions per call, then wait for the answers before asking the next batch.

Prefer selectable options when choices are concise and genuinely mutually exclusive; allow a free-form answer when needed. Do not present a long questionnaire in normal chat. If no native input tool is available, ask one concise blocking question at a time. For non-blocking gaps, state the assumption and continue.

Build continuity around a specific travel day. Verify live facts; do not rely on remembered schedules, border rules, or compensation policies.

## Normalise the itinerary

Ask for dated segments, local time zones, operators, booking relationships, terminals or stations, check-in and boarding cutoffs, baggage path, destination transport, accommodation, medication, mobility, dependents, connectivity, payment, and insurance.

Rewrite every segment in local date and time. Mark separate tickets, terminal changes, immigration, security, baggage reclaim and recheck, and the last practical onward service.

## Audit the buffers

For each connection, list processes the traveller must complete and the evidence used for timing. Never call a connection safe solely because scheduled arrival precedes departure.

## Protect critical access

Keep verified documents, medication, keys, payment, contacts, and one essential change personally accessible. Save tickets where permitted, itinerary, addresses, maps, operator policies, and independently verified contact routes offline.

## Write failure branches

Use [DISRUPTION-PLAYBOOK.md](DISRUPTION-PLAYBOOK.md), then compress the selected branches into [OFFLINE-TRAVEL-BRIEF.md](OFFLINE-TRAVEL-BRIEF.md).

Set explicit points to recheck current operator and government information before departure and during the journey.

Do not guarantee entry, boarding, compensation, or connection feasibility. Prioritise safety, documents, medication, shelter, and communication before reimbursement administration.
