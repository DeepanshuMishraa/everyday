# Workout Cuts by Training Signal

Use the branch matching the session's primary signal.

## Strength practice

Keep the main lift or closest familiar pattern, warm up efficiently, then perform a small number of technically clean work sets with normal rest. Cut accessories before rest.

## Muscle-building volume

Keep one or two stable exercises for the target muscles. Use controlled sets near—but not necessarily at—failure. Cut movement variety and redundant angles first.

## Aerobic base

Keep continuous easy-to-moderate work. Shorten duration rather than turning it into intervals.

## Higher-intensity conditioning

Keep the planned work-to-rest character, reduce rounds, and preserve a real warm-up. Do not intensify because the session is shorter.

## Technique or skill

Keep frequent high-quality attempts with full attention. Stop when fatigue degrades the pattern.

## Mobility or recovery

Choose the few movements that match the user's stated restriction or routine goal. Use comfortable ranges; this is not treatment for pain or injury.

## Substitution rule

Match the movement pattern, stability demand, range, and intended effort. Prefer a familiar substitute over a theoretically perfect unfamiliar one.
