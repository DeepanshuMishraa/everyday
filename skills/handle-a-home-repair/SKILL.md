# Handle a Home Repair

## Interaction

When missing information materially changes the outcome and the host exposes a dedicated user-input or question tool, use that tool. Ask no more than three short, related questions per call, then wait for the answers before asking the next batch.

Prefer selectable options when choices are concise and genuinely mutually exclusive; allow a free-form answer when needed. Do not present a long questionnaire in normal chat. If no native input tool is available, ask one concise blocking question at a time. For non-blocking gaps, state the assumption and continue.

This skill performs **first response**, not remote diagnosis or repair instruction.

## 1. Screen the hazard

Ask first about gas or fuel smell, smoke, fire, sparking, heat, shock, carbon-monoxide alarms, rapid water ingress, ceiling movement, structural cracking, or anyone feeling unwell.

Read [HAZARD-TRIAGE.md](HAZARD-TRIAGE.md) whenever any signal is present or the system involved is unclear. Stop ordinary troubleshooting at the first emergency or specialist-only branch.

**Done when:** the user has either moved to safety and contacted appropriate local help, or the issue is credible for low-risk observation.

## 2. Describe, do not diagnose

Capture:

- what changed and where
- when it began and whether it is worsening
- what is visible, audible, smelled, or displayed
- what was happening immediately before it began
- photos, short recordings, model numbers, or meter readings that are safe to collect

Keep observations separate from the user's theory.

## 3. Limit the problem

Suggest only actions available without opening panels, climbing, touching exposed wiring, handling gas, defeating a safety device, entering standing water, or disturbing a structure. Examples include moving possessions away, placing a container under a minor drip, or switching off an appliance using its normal control when safe.

## 4. Choose a response tier

Choose one:

- emergency or utility response
- urgent licensed professional
- landlord or property manager
- routine service booking
- monitored observation with a specific time and escalation trigger

Explain the evidence that selected the tier. Ask the user's country before giving tenancy, code, or emergency-number information.

Use [REPAIR-BRIEF-FORMAT.md](REPAIR-BRIEF-FORMAT.md) for the handoff.
