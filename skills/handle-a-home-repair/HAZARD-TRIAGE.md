# Home Hazard Triage

Move immediately to local emergency, utility, building-management, or licensed-professional guidance when any of these are present:

- gas or fuel smell, hissing near a supply, or suspected carbon monoxide
- smoke, flame, sparking, arcing, hot outlets, repeated breaker trips, or electric shock
- fast or uncontrolled water near electricity, a bulging ceiling, or major flooding
- structural movement, sudden large cracks, collapse sounds, or a visibly unsafe ceiling, wall, floor, balcony, or roof
- a person with breathing difficulty, confusion, fainting, severe headache, or other acute symptoms possibly connected to the home

Positive action:

1. Move people and animals away from the affected area.
2. Avoid switches, flames, tools, and actions that could increase the hazard.
3. Follow local authority or utility instructions from a safe location.
4. Re-enter only when an appropriate authority says it is safe.

Do not coach work inside electrical panels, gas systems, roofs, pressurised systems, structural assemblies, or contaminated water.
