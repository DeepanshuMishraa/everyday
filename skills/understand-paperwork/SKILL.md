# Understand Paperwork

## Interaction

When missing information materially changes the outcome and the host exposes a dedicated user-input or question tool, use that tool. Ask no more than three short, related questions per call, then wait for the answers before asking the next batch.

Prefer selectable options when choices are concise and genuinely mutually exclusive; allow a free-form answer when needed. Do not present a long questionnaire in normal chat. If no native input tool is available, ask one concise blocking question at a time. For non-blocking gaps, state the assumption and continue.

Turn the document into an **action brief** while preserving the difference between what it says and what you infer.

## Get the whole document

Ask for clear text or images of every page, the envelope or delivery date when deadlines may depend on it, attachments, prior correspondence, and the user's country or jurisdiction when relevant. Remind the user to redact identifiers that are not needed.

Stop if pages, dates, or referenced attachments are missing and those omissions could change the action.

## Extract before explaining

Record exactly:

- issuer, recipient, date, subject, and reference number
- requested actions
- stated deadlines and how they are defined
- required evidence, signatures, fees, and submission channels
- consequences the document itself states

Never manufacture a deadline from tone or context.

## Translate carefully

Explain dense language in ordinary words. Label each statement as:

- **Document says** — direct and supported
- **Likely meaning** — a reasonable interpretation
- **Need to ask** — ambiguity only the issuer or a qualified professional can resolve

Use [DOCUMENT-BRIEF-FORMAT.md](DOCUMENT-BRIEF-FORMAT.md) for the result.

## Boundary

Organise and explain; do not decide legal rights, predict an authority's decision, or tell the user what regulated choice to make. Recommend qualified local help when court action, immigration status, penalties, loss of rights, or substantial money is involved.
