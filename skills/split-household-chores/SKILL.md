# Split Household Chores Fairly

## Interaction

When missing information materially changes the outcome and the host exposes a dedicated user-input or question tool, use that tool. Ask no more than three short, related questions per call, then wait for the answers before asking the next batch.

Prefer selectable options when choices are concise and genuinely mutually exclusive; allow a free-form answer when needed. Do not present a long questionnaire in normal chat. If no native input tool is available, ask one concise blocking question at a time. For non-blocking gaps, state the assumption and continue.

Assign **ownership**, not help. Ownership runs from noticing through planning, supplies, doing, and follow-up.

## Inventory the whole job

Ask who lives in the household, their schedules and capacities, recurring responsibilities, current ownership, paid help, and the conflict that keeps recurring.

Use [INVISIBLE-WORK.md](INVISIBLE-WORK.md) to expose work hidden behind the visible final action.

## Define done

For each responsibility, agree on an observable minimum standard, frequency or trigger, and any time sensitivity. Do not assume one person's unspoken standard is the household standard.

## Assign ownership

Give one person primary ownership for each outcome. Assistance can be shared, but the owner remembers and closes the loop. Compare the total load by time, frequency, cognitive load, unpleasantness, flexibility, and each person's external capacity. Fair need not mean identical.

## Design exceptions

Specify how cover is requested and confirmed during illness, travel, deadlines, or capacity changes. A handoff is complete only when the new owner explicitly accepts it.

Use [HOUSEHOLD-AGREEMENT-FORMAT.md](HOUSEHOLD-AGREEMENT-FORMAT.md) for a two- to four-week trial. Review the system, not a prosecution log of every miss.

Account for disability, care, age, and safe child participation. Do not use a chore agreement to normalise coercive control, financial abuse, or unsafe conditions.
