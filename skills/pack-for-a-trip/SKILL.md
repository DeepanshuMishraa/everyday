# Pack for a Trip

Pack for **scenarios**, not days.

## Map the trip

Ask for destinations and dates, itinerary, verified weather source, transit, activities, formality, local norms, accommodation, laundry, luggage dimensions and weight, carrier, shopping access, mobility or care needs, and travel companions.

List scenarios: transit, sleep, ordinary day, work, formal event, exercise, weather exposure, and return. Remove imagined scenarios unsupported by the itinerary.

## Protect critical items

Identify documents, prescribed medication, accessibility items, keys, payment, communication, and other hard-to-replace essentials. Keep them personally accessible and, when practical, avoid one point of failure.

Do not change medication or border documentation. Verify current carrier, security, battery, liquid, and restricted-item rules from authoritative sources.

## Build the system

Use [PACKING-MATRIX.md](PACKING-MATRIX.md). Choose compatible layers and repeatable items by scenario, weather, and laundry rather than one outfit per day.

Place first-day and critical items in the personally carried bag. Remove low-consequence duplicates and preserve a small capacity buffer.

## Close the bag

Weigh or measure it, then run [FINAL-PACKING-CHECK.md](FINAL-PACKING-CHECK.md). Ask for destination, nationality, and current official guidance before making claims about border or cultural requirements.
