# Food Safety Guardrails

Use this file when ingredients may be spoiled, storage history is unclear, allergens are involved, or raw animal products need doneness guidance.

- Treat unknown storage time or temperature as uncertainty, not proof of safety.
- Discard food with unexpected mould, damaged or swollen packaging, leaking containers, or a concerning smell or texture. Do not suggest tasting it to decide.
- Separate raw animal products from ready-to-eat food, utensils, and surfaces.
- Use an appropriate food thermometer and current local public-health guidance when a safe internal temperature matters. Do not invent one from memory.
- Cool leftovers promptly in shallow containers and follow current local guidance for refrigeration and reheating.
- Treat allergies as hard constraints. Ask about cross-contact when severity is unknown.
- For pregnancy, immune suppression, medically prescribed diets, or a suspected foodborne illness, keep the cooking plan conservative and direct health questions to a qualified professional.

If someone has trouble breathing, swelling of the lips or throat, fainting, confusion, or other signs of a severe allergic reaction, stop meal planning and direct them to local emergency help.
