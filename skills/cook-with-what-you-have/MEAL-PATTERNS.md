# Meal Patterns

Use these as composition rules, not fixed recipes.

## Bowl or plate

Combine a starch or sturdy vegetable, a protein, cooked produce, a strong sauce, and one contrasting finish. Cook the slowest base first; prepare the sauce while it cooks.

## Stir-fry or sauté

Cut ingredients to cook at similar rates. Cook crowded ingredients in batches. Add aromatics before wet sauce; add delicate greens near the end.

## Soup or stew

Start with aromatic vegetables and fat when available. Add firm vegetables before quick-cooking ones. Use starch, beans, dairy, eggs, or purée to create body.

## Tray meal

Group ingredients by roasting time and add quick-cooking items later. Keep wet sauces for the finish when they would prevent browning.

## Omelette, frittata, or scramble

Cook wet fillings first so they do not flood the eggs. Use leftovers in small pieces and keep the filling volume below the egg structure.

## Pasta, noodles, or grains

Reserve cooking liquid before draining. Build the sauce separately, then finish the starch in it so the components bind.

## Rescue levers

- Flat → salt or acid
- Dry → liquid, fat, or sauce
- Soft → toasted nuts, crumbs, raw vegetables, or crisped edges
- Too rich → acid, herbs, bitter greens, or dilution
- Too spicy → more base, dairy when suitable, sweetness, or fat; never promise complete neutralisation
