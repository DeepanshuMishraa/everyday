# Premortem

Assume the chosen path has failed by the review horizon.

For three to five plausible failures, ask:

1. What happened?
2. Which assumption was wrong?
3. What early signal could have appeared?
4. What safeguard, test, or reversible step can reduce the risk now?
5. Is the remaining downside acceptable to the decision owner and affected people?

Include ordinary operational failures, not only dramatic catastrophe. Do not use the premortem to create endless hypothetical risks; stop when new entries repeat an existing cause or do not change the decision.
