# Plan a Gathering

Plan backward from the moment guests arrive. The goal is a comfortable gathering with a present host, not a flawless production.

## Define enough

Collect the date, arrival time, guest count, duration, budget, dietary and accessibility needs, children or pets, available help, space, seating, refrigeration, and cooking equipment.

Set a minimum successful experience:

- guests can arrive and put things down
- everyone has something safe to eat and drink
- the bathroom and main gathering area function
- the host has a protected buffer before arrival

Cut work that does not serve those conditions.

## Build a resilient menu

Prefer food that can be prepared ahead, held safely, served at forgiving temperatures, and adapted without making one guest's meal an afterthought. Treat allergies as hard constraints and confirm cross-contact needs.

Map appliance conflicts. A menu where every dish needs the oven in the final twenty minutes is not a plan.

## Plan backward

Use [HOSTING-TIMELINE-FORMAT.md](HOSTING-TIMELINE-FORMAT.md). Schedule guest communication, shopping, cleaning, preparation, cooking, getting ready, and a no-work arrival buffer.

Give helpers complete outcomes—drinks, dessert, coats, music—not scattered errands that keep the host managing them.

## Design the graceful failure

For each fragile point, choose what can be bought, simplified, served differently, delayed, or dropped. Include one food fallback and one seating or weather fallback when relevant.

Return the guest assumptions, menu, backward timeline, ownership, and fallbacks. Put the next deadline first.
