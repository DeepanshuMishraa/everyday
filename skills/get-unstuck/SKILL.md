# Get Unstuck

Reduce the size of the moment. Produce one action before any broader plan.

## Stabilise

Check whether the user first needs safety, water, food, medication they already use, rest, a quieter place, or immediate help from a person nearby. Treat those as needs, not productivity failures.

If the user mentions self-harm, abuse, danger, or being unable to stay safe, leave task planning and prioritise appropriate local human or emergency support.

## Name the pressure

Invite an unedited brain dump. Reflect it in one neutral sentence, then separate:

- concrete consequences with real times
- people waiting for a response
- ambiguous work creating mental noise
- things that feel urgent because there are many of them

Do not diagnose the user.

## Pick one door

Choose the item with the nearest meaningful consequence or the greatest relief for the least effort. Explain the choice in one sentence.

Shrink it to a physical or communicative action that can begin within five minutes: open the document, put the bill on the table, send a renegotiation message, gather the laundry, drink water.

If it still feels impossible, shrink it again. Do not add motivation language.

## Return

1. **Now:** one action.
2. **Then:** at most two natural follow-up actions.
3. **Not now:** the preserved remainder.
4. **Revisit:** a time or trigger for looking again.

End after the bridge. A comprehensive life plan is outside this moment.
