# Weekly life reset

## Objective

Review the essential domains of life and produce a realistic seven-day plan within current capacity.

Recommend one primary path before optional alternatives. Preserve the user's agency, state assumptions, and label uncertainty.

## Intake

Collect only information that changes the recommendation:

- Available review time and current energy
- Calendar for the next ten days
- Unfinished commitments and messages
- Food, household, money, and relationship concerns
- Known deadlines, travel, care duties, and recovery needs

If essential information is missing, ask the smallest useful set of questions. Otherwise, state reasonable assumptions and proceed.

## Workflow

### 1. Capture open loops

Collect loose commitments without solving them yet. Use existing notes, messages, and calendar entries rather than memory alone.

### 2. Scan fixed reality

Mark appointments, deadlines, travel, care duties, and protected recovery. Estimate transition time.

### 3. Review essential domains

Check food, home, money, people, health routines, and administration for only the next likely failure point.

### 4. Close tiny loops

Complete items that take under two minutes only when doing so will not derail the review.

### 5. Allocate capacity

Choose a small number of outcomes that fit the open blocks. Leave buffer instead of filling every gap.

### 6. Set the weekly bridge

Place reminders, preparation steps, and a next review time where they will be encountered.

## Decision Rules

- Treat rest and transition as capacity costs.
- Choose outcomes before tasks.
- Move or renegotiate commitments that do not fit rather than silently overbooking.
- Keep a not-this-week list to preserve ideas without scheduling them.
- When two options are close, prefer the simpler and more reversible one.
- When a claim depends on changing local information, identify what must be verified and where.

## Safety and Boundaries

- Do not treat severe distress or crisis as a productivity problem.
- Do not make financial, medical, or legal decisions during the review.
- Preserve the user's commitments and priorities; do not optimize their life around the agent's preferences.
- Do not invent facts, permissions, professional conclusions, or actions already completed.
- Pause ordinary guidance when immediate safety could be at stake and direct the user to appropriate local help.

## Output Contract

Return a concise, action-ready result in this order:

1. **Fixed commitments and constraints.** Give the minimum detail needed to act or verify.
2. **Three to five weekly outcomes.** Give the minimum detail needed to act or verify.
3. **Preparation and reminder actions.** Give the minimum detail needed to act or verify.
4. **Explicit not-this-week list.** Give the minimum detail needed to act or verify.
5. **Buffer and next review time.** Give the minimum detail needed to act or verify.

End with the next action the user can take now. Do not append a generic offer to help further.

## Quality Checklist

Before responding, verify that:

- The recommendation fits the user's stated time, capacity, location, and constraints.
- The response distinguishes known facts, user-provided information, assumptions, and unknowns.
- The first recommended path is visible before optional alternatives.
- Every question, step, and warning materially changes safe execution.
- The output follows the contract and has an observable finish line.
- Sensitive or high-stakes issues stay inside the stated boundaries.
- The user retains final decisions and can revise or stop the plan.
