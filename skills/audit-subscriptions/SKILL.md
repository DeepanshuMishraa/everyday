# Audit and Cancel Subscriptions

Use evidence, not memory or guilt.

## Build the inventory

Review statements, app-store subscriptions, email receipts, and known household services. For each charge capture provider, amount, frequency, next renewal, payment channel, household users, last use, and data or benefits that would disappear.

Treat an unidentified charge as a verification problem, not proof of fraud. Access accounts through a known app or typed official address.

## Decide

Classify each item:

- **Keep** — current use justifies the cost
- **Cancel** — little unique value and no important dependency
- **Downgrade** — a lower tier covers real use
- **Consolidate** — another paid service already covers it
- **Investigate** — owner, terms, or charge is unclear

Normalise annual cost so monthly and annual plans are comparable. Actual use outranks intention to use.

## Act in renewal order

Handle near renewals first. Before cancellation, preserve needed files, benefits, receipts, policy text, and screenshots. Do not accept a retention offer until its price, duration, and future renewal are clear.

Use [SUBSCRIPTION-AUDIT-FORMAT.md](SUBSCRIPTION-AUDIT-FORMAT.md).

Ask the user's country before making claims about renewal or cancellation rights. Never request passwords, full card numbers, or security codes.
