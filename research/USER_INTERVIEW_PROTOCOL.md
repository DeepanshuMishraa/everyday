# User interview protocol

Do not expand the catalog beyond 30 workflows until this round is complete.

## Participants

Recruit 5–8 people who already use an AI assistant for at least one recurring non-work task. Include a mix of confident and occasional AI users. Do not recruit only friends who already understand SKILL.md.

## Research question

When do people need a reusable workflow instead of a fresh answer, and does Everyday help them act with less reconstruction and uncertainty?

## Session

Run 30-minute, one-to-one sessions. Ask for permission before recording.

1. “Tell me about the last everyday task where you knew you had learned a better process but could not remember it.”
2. “What did you do next?”
3. “Show me how you would find help for that situation today.”
4. Give the participant Everyday without explaining its structure. Ask them to find and start a relevant workflow.
5. Ask: “What did you expect this to do?” and “What, if anything, would you save or return to?”
6. Ask them to explain the difference between reading, saving, and installing a workflow.
7. End with: “How disappointed would you be if Everyday no longer existed?” Do not treat this answer as PMF evidence until the participant has used it in real life.

Do not lead participants toward agent installation. Observe their vocabulary and mental model.

## Record for each session

- Triggering situation
- Current workaround
- Search terms used
- First-click expectation
- Task success and time to first relevant workflow
- Confusion or hesitation
- Whether they started, saved, or installed
- Stated return trigger
- Missing workflow requests

## Expansion gate

Add a new workflow only when all conditions hold:

1. At least three independent users describe the same recurring situation, or zero-result analytics and interviews support the same gap.
2. A reusable procedure offers more value than a one-off answer.
3. The outcome is observable.
4. Safety boundaries can be stated clearly.
5. Existing categories or search synonyms cannot solve the discovery problem.

Publish findings as themes, not participant profiles. Never invent quotes, demand, or PMF conclusions.
